How to Make a PC

Embarking on the journey of building your own PC grants you unparalleled control over its performance and aesthetic appeal. To guide you through this process, we've assembled a comprehensive, step-by-step walkthrough:

  1. Ensuring Component Compatibility: The linchpin of a successful build is verifying that all your chosen parts will function harmoniously. For this, we enthusiastically recommend utilizing PC Part Picker. This exceptional tool not only guarantees compatibility but also aids in discovering the most advantageous deals.
  2. CPU Installation: Exercise utmost care when handling this critical component. Begin by gently lifting the lever on the CPU socket to a 90Â° angle. Precisely align the triangle marking on the CPU with its counterpart on the socket. Carefully seat the CPU straight down into the socket; it should fit without any applied pressure. Finally, secure the CPU by pressing down the lever, ensuring it rests perfectly flush.
  3. CPU Cooler Application: A pivotal step in maintaining optimal thermal performance. Apply a minuscule, pea-sized drop of thermal paste to the center of the CPU's surface. During cooler selection, meticulously verify its size and weight constraints relative to your case and motherboard. An excessively large or heavy cooler harbors the potential to inflict damage upon the motherboard.
  4. RAM Installation: A relatively straightforward procedure. Align the notch on the RAM module with the corresponding slot on the motherboard. Prior to inserting the module, unlock the latches on the RAM slot. Exert even pressure on both ends of the RAM stick until an audible click confirms its secure placement. Subsequently, lock the latches.
  5. Motherboard Mounting: The time has come to secure the motherboard within the confines of your case. Affix it to the standoffs provided with your case. Given that this stage involves connecting various cables, we advise consulting online tutorials if you require guidance.
  6. Storage Drive Installation: Your case and/or motherboard will feature dedicated bays or mounts for accommodating your storage devices (SSDs and HDDs). Securely fasten your drives within these designated locations.
  7. Graphics Card Installation: The graphics card (GPU) is installed into the primary PCIe slot on your motherboard. Should you encounter difficulty in locating this slot, please refer to your motherboard's manual for assistance.
  8. Cable Management: Employing effective cable management techniques not only enhances the visual appeal of your PC but also promotes optimal airflow. Most cases incorporate routing channels and tie-down points to facilitate this. We recommend exploring online cable management guides tailored to your specific case model or comparable designs for inspiration.
  9. Initial Power-On: The moment of truth approaches! Power on your PC to ascertain whether all components are functioning as expected. For this preliminary testing phase, it is prudent to keep the side panels detached to facilitate easier troubleshooting. Exercise caution to ensure that no foreign objects come into contact with the PC's internal components, as this could pose an electrical hazard.
  10. Operating System Installation: This process necessitates the use of either a USB drive or installation media. Given that the installation procedure varies depending on the chosen operating system (e.g., Windows, Linux), we recommend consulting online resources for detailed, OS-specific instructions.
  11. Discover More at Atom: We are dedicated to providing you with the resources necessary to embark on your PC building journey. Keep an eye out for more in-depth tips and guides on our website!

For comprehensive instructions and troubleshooting assistance, we strongly encourage you to consult your component manuals and reputable online resources. Remember, PC building is a dynamic learning experience, so embrace the process, exercise patience, and savor the satisfaction of creating your own custom-built machine!
